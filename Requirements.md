SocialOps — What It Does
SocialOps is a social media management platform that lets individuals and teams plan, create, publish, and measure content across multiple social platforms from a single workspace.

Authentication
Users can create an account with their name, email, and password. Existing users log in with email/password, with a password visibility toggle and a forgot password option. Both login and register flows lead into the main dashboard.

Dashboard
The dashboard is the home screen after login. It greets the user by name and gives a real-time overview of their social media performance. It shows total follower count with a percentage growth trend. An AI Smart Insight card surfaces an automatically generated recommendation — for example, identifying the best time of day to post based on when engagement peaks — with a confidence score and a one-click "Apply" button to act on it. An audience breakdown panel shows the top countries where the audience is located, each with a percentage share. An activity density panel visualises how frequently posts go out across different times of the week. Below these panels sits a Recent Performance section showing the last four published posts, each displaying its thumbnail, content type (Reel, Carousel, or Story), publish date, like count, and comment count. A persistent floating button on every logged-in screen opens the Create Post flow instantly.

Create Post
The create post experience is a full-screen composer. On the left side, the user fills in a post title and writes a caption. They upload images or videos into a media gallery, with the ability to remove individual media items. They then select which platforms to publish to — Instagram, Twitter, and LinkedIn are available as toggleable options. They choose the content type: Post, Reel, or Story. A scheduling section lets them toggle on scheduled posting and pick a specific date and time for the content to go live. On the right side, a live preview panel renders exactly how the post will look on the selected platform in real time as the user types, with a toggle to switch between mobile and desktop preview modes. At the bottom, the user can either save the post as a draft, or hit Publish to send it out.

Analytics
The analytics screen gives a full performance overview across all connected platforms. Four headline KPI cards display total followers, total impressions, engagement rate, and post frequency — each with a percentage change indicator showing whether the metric is trending up or down compared to the previous period. A large Growth & Engagement chart plots performance over a four-week period, with tabs to switch the view between Engagement, Reach, and Conversions. A Network Distribution panel shows what percentage of total performance comes from each platform (Instagram, TikTok, LinkedIn) visualised as segmented bars. An Audience DNA panel shows the primary age group of the audience on a circular chart, alongside a breakdown of top regions by percentage. The user can export the analytics data or share the report directly from this screen.

Scheduler
The scheduler gives a structured view of what is planned and when. The user can switch between a weekly and a monthly view of their content queue. The main area shows a timeline for the current day with time markers (e.g. 9:00 AM, 11:30 AM, 2:00 PM). Each time slot either shows a scheduled post card — with its title, platform, status (Active or Scheduled), thumbnail, and hashtags — or appears as an empty dashed slot that the user can click to create a new post for that time. A three-dot overflow menu on each scheduled post allows further actions. The right sidebar shows three things: a Queue Health card displaying how many posts are scheduled for the week and an optimisation score as a circular progress ring; a Drafts panel listing saved drafts by title and creation time with a link to view all drafts; and a trending alert that notifies the user when one of their posts is currently trending on a platform.

Navigation & Global Elements
A top navigation bar is present across all screens once logged in. It contains links to Dashboard, Analytics, and Scheduler, a search bar for finding metrics, a notifications bell, and the user's profile avatar. A footer on every screen carries links to Privacy Policy, Terms of Service, and Help Center.
